# Matched Population is the comparison population for all model-vs-ADP evaluation

Every pre-2026-07-04 "model beats ADP" claim compared the model's Spearman ρ over the full backtest population (every eligible Veteran, including undraftable depth players) against ADP's ρ over only the ADP-matched subset. These are different populations, and the full-population number is inflated by the trivial sub-task of ranking stars above bench players. Measured apples-to-apples on the Matched Population, ADP beats the model at all four positions (see `docs/research/model-vs-adp-matched-population.md`).

Matched-population Spearman ρ vs. the Answer Key, per position, is the metric this ADR establishes. The Answer Key is actual season **total** fantasy points (not PPG): total points are what win leagues and what ADP itself prices in, and they force the Games-Played Estimate to earn its keep rather than letting the model compete on a points-if-playing basis ADP never gets to use. Full-population ρ may still be reported for context but must never be cited as evidence of edge over ADP.

**Amended 2026-07-05**: this metric is no longer the project's headline — it measures overall rank agreement with actual outcomes, which isn't the same question as "are the model's disagreements with ADP trustworthy." That question, and the actual v2 win condition, now belongs to Disagreement Edge (ADR-0014). Matched-population Spearman remains a supporting metric, and the Matched Population itself (defined here) remains the comparison population Disagreement Edge and tier accuracy are both computed on — that part of this decision is unchanged.
