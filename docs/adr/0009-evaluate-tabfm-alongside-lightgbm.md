# Evaluate a tabular foundation model (TabFM) alongside LightGBM before committing to one for v1

ADR-0002 chose LightGBM as the modeling approach. We're keeping that as the working baseline but also training a tabular foundation model (TabPFN-style pretrained TabFM) on the same per-position splits, no strong hypothesis yet — just an apples-to-apples comparison via the Walk-Forward Backtest and ADP Benchmark before locking in one approach for v1. Per-position quantile outputs (P10/P50/P90) stay the comparison bar for both, since boom/bust risk still matters as much as expected value. If TabFM doesn't clearly beat LightGBM on the backtest, LightGBM remains the v1 choice and this becomes a dead end rather than a rewrite.

**Outcome (2026-07-04)**: the comparison never ran. TabPFNRegressor hard-errors above a 10,000-row training cap (`ignore_pretraining_limits=True` is the only override, and it's explicitly unsupported by the library); the pipeline's real RB training data alone, on the full expanding window since 2006, is already ~23,000 rows and only grows. Rather than force an unsupported override or subsample around the cap, the owner chose to skip TabFM entirely and proceed LightGBM-only for both v1 and v2 (per PRD #11). The `model_backend="tabfm"` capability remains built and tested (`ff/src/ff_model/tabfm_model.py`) but is intentionally not wired into `run_backtest.py`/`__main__.py`. Issue #8 (TabFM comparison model) is closed as won't-do on this basis; if TabFM is revisited later, the row-cap question needs an answer first.
