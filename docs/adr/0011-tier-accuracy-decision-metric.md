# Tier accuracy is the decision metric; Spearman remains the development metric

Within a tier of similar players, true finish order is dominated by injury luck and TD variance — noise no forecaster can call — so exact-rank metrics partly grade coin flips. The predictable, draft-actionable structure is tier membership and tier boundaries ("take the last guy before the tier breaks"). v2 therefore adds a **tier accuracy** scoreboard: per position, Tiers are derived from each season's *actual* outcomes (clustered gaps in total points or ~12-player buckets ≈ one draft round, targeting roughly 6–8 tiers per position — coarse enough to stop grading noise, fine enough to stay hard), and both the model's list and ADP's list are scored on how often they place each player in his true tier (off-by-one tracked as a secondary).

This is not a lowered bar: ADP is graded on the same coarse scale, so tiers change the granularity of the contest, not the opponent. The two metrics have different jobs — matched-population Spearman (ADR-0010) is the sensitive dial watched during development, since small real improvements often won't move tier counts; tier accuracy is the human-meaningful win condition ("the model called the S-tier better than the crowd").
